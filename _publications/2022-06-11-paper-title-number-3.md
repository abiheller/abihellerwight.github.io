Citation: Beadle, J. N., Gifford, A., & **Heller, A**. (2022). A Narrative Review of Loneliness and Brain Health in Older Adults: Implications of COVID-19. Current Behavioral Neuroscience Reports, 9(3), 73–83. https://doi.org/10.1007/s40473-021-00237-6

Abstract: This narrative review highlights important factors contributing to loneliness in older adults prior to and during the COVID-19 pandemic and effects on brain health. We characterize risk factors for loneliness in older adulthood and the impact of COVID-19. Furthermore, we discuss the implications of loneliness for older adults’ brain health. Understanding the multifactorial causes of loneliness in different subpopulations of older adults both before and during the COVID-19 pandemic will provide insights for the development of interventions targeted to reduce loneliness in older adults based on their specific risk factors.
