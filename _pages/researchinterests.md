Memory Ability Across the Lifespan
======

I will be attending the 2025 Dallas Aging and Cognition Conference from February 20-22. My poster, titled "Relational Memory Across the Lifespan: Time, Space, and Association from 8 to 80 Years Old", presents research using the task originally reported in [Konkel et al, 2008](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/neuro.09.015.2008/full) to measure relational memory across the lifespan. Understanding how memory develops and declines could offer valuable insights into disease processes, such as Alzheimer's, and the brain structures involved in memory. Additionally, the task we use is applicable to individuals of all ages (8-80 years) and various cognitive abilities (including lesion patients and those with amnestic mild cognitive impairment), making comparisons across cohorts more robust. Our lab has recently published an analysis of this task in healthy younger and older adults, and we are currently preparing a detailed report on childhood relational memory development (ages 8-13).

If you stopped by my poster, I am so glad we were able to meet and chat. Here are the references from my poster if you are interested in reading more: 
* Konkel, A. (2008). Hippocampal Amnesia Impairs All Manner of Relational Memory. Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 2. 
* Sexton, S., Behm, L., Rose, J., Phipps, C., Ramirez, M., Heller-Wight, A., Wilhelm, A., Armbruster, E., Nagengast, C., Warren, D. (in press). Measuring Relational Memory in Younger and Older Adults. Frontiers in Cognition, 4.
