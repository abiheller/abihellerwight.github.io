About Me
======
My name is Abi Heller-Wight (formerly Heller) and I am a 4th year PhD candidate at the University of Nebraska Medical Center in the Department of Neurological Sciences, as part of the [Medical Scientist Training Program (MSTP)](https://www.unmc.edu/com/admissions/academic-programs/md-phd-program/index.html). 

I am completing my PhD work in [Dr. Dave Warren's lab](https://www.unmc.edu/neurologicalsciences/research/wnl/index.html) studying human memory systems in development and aging. My dissertation work focuses on the influence of physical activity and fitness on the development of cognition (specifically memory) and the brain structures/networks that support these abilities. 

Clinically, I am interested in risk factors (cardiovascular disease, genetic profiles, frailty) that influence the development of brain disease, particularly in aging populations. I hope to match into a neurology residency program once I finish the MSTP program.

Prior to joining the MSTP program at UNMC, I attended the University of Nebraska Omaha and conducted research in the Aging Brain and Emotion lab, mentored by [Dr. Janelle Beadle](https://www.unomaha.edu/college-of-arts-and-sciences/neuroscience/about-us/directory/janelle-beadle.php). During my time in the ABE lab, I learned about empathy and aging, focusing on characteristics that make people (older adults, caregivers, astronauts, and everyone in between) behave more prosocially. My honors thesis investigated the underlying neural mechanisms of positivity and empathy using functional Near Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS).
